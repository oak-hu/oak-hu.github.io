① Trick map — the one move that makes each proof go

Foundations / engines
Estimation (ML) lemma 2.2.8(4) Write ∮f = reiθ, project f onto the eiθ direction → real triangle inequality. ⇒ |∮γf| ≤ sup|f|·ℓ(γ). Powers every "→0" estimate.
FTC for path integrals 2.2.13 / conv 2.2.16 Chain rule: d/dt F(γ(t)) = f(γ(t))γ′(t). Converse: F(z)=∮ along any path z₀→z is well-defined ⟺ loops vanish. ⇒ primitive ⟺ ∮=0.
Winding number 2.5.1/2/4 Lift γ=|γ|e2πi a (uniform-cts → glue local args); integer-valued + cts ⇒ locally constant. I(γ,z₀)=a(1)−a(0)=1/2πi ∮ dz/(z−z₀).
Cauchy-type integrals are analytic Prop 2.5.6 — THE engine Geometric series 1/(z−w)=Σ wⁿ/zn+1; |f|≤M on compact γ* ⇒ M-test ⇒ uniform ⇒ swap Σ↔∮. Makes ∮f/(z−w) a power series in w. Drives Taylor & Laurent.
The spine
Cauchy–Goursat (triangles) 2.3.1 Bisection: split T into 4, keep the worst, nest to a point; differentiability (ε-form) kills the estimate. ⇒ ∮∂Tf=0. Base case of everything.
Cauchy on star-like U 2.4.x Build a primitive F(z)=∮ from the star centre; Goursat on triangles ⇒ F′=f ⇒ ∮=0.
Deformation theorem 2.4.7 Chop the homotopy square into cells, each landing in a disc where a primitive exists; telescope adjacent primitives. "Locally, primitives always exist."
Cauchy on s.c. domains 2.4.9 Null-homotopic ⇒ ∮=0; with 2.2.16 ⇒ every holo f on a simply-connected domain has a primitive.
γ ≃ small circle 2.5.10 (topology, unproved) The topological licence to shrink any simple contour to a tiny circle — used inside the CIF proof.
Cauchy's Integral Formula 3.1.1 (+3.1.3) Deform γ to a tiny circle about w; ∮(f(z)−f(w))/(z−w) →0 (bounded integrand, est. lemma), leaving f(w)·I(γ,w). Multi-curve: add cuts to make s.c.
Taylor: holo ⇒ analytic Cor 3.3.2 Feed CIF into Prop 2.5.6: the CIF integrand is a Cauchy-type integral ⇒ power series in w. ⇒ holo ⟺ analytic ⟺ C∞; f(n)(z₀)=n!/2πi ∮ f/(z−z₀)n+1.
Falls out of CIF + Taylor
Liouville 3.3.4 Two-centre CIF: f(w)−f(0)=1/2πi ∮ w·f/(z(z−w)); est. lemma ≤ M|w|/(R−|w|) → 0 as R→∞.
Fundamental thm of algebra 3.3.6 Else 1/p entire; leading term ⇒ |p|≥½|z|ⁿ ⇒ 1/p bounded ⇒ Liouville ⇒ const. ✗. (Always: leading term dominates.)
Morera 3.3.8 cts + all triangle/loop ∮=0 ⇒ primitive F (2.2.16); F holo ⇒ C∞ (Taylor!) ⇒ f=F′ holo. Converse of Cauchy.
Loc-unif limits stay holo 3.3.12 On a ball ∮f=lim ∮fn=0 (uniform on compact γ*) ⇒ Morera.
Identity theorem 3.4.3 Zeros isolated-or-everywhere (Taylor factor (z−z₀)kg₁, g₁(z₀)≠0); clopen + connected ⇒ agreement on a set with an interior limit point spreads globally.
Riemann removable sing. 3.5.21 h=(z−z₀)²f extends holo with a double zero (diff-quotient → 0 by boundedness); Taylor ⇒ f extends. ⇒ bounded ⟺ removable.
Casorati–Weierstrass 3.5.25 Miss a value by a margin ⇒ 1/(f−z₀) bounded ⇒ Riemann ⇒ pole, not essential. ✗ ⇒ image dense.
Laurent's theorem 3.5.9 Two-circle CIF (3.1.3) on the annulus; geometric series outward & inward + M-test ⇒ f=Σ−∞∞ cn(z−z₀)ⁿ.
Residue theorem 3.5.27 Subtract all principal parts → holo remainder, ∮=0 (deformation); only the c−1/(z−a) terms survive (others have primitives). ⇒ ∮=2πi Σ I(γ,a)Resaf.
Argument-principle family
Argument principle 3.6.2 Residue thm on f′/f (residues = orders ±k, Lem 3.6.1). N−P = 1/2πi ∮ f′/f = I(f∘γ, 0) = winding of the image curve about 0.
Rouché 3.6.3 |f|>|g| on γ* ⇒ apply arg principle to f and f+g separately; difference = 1/2πi ∮ h′/h, h=(f+g)/f, and h(γ)⊂B(1,1)⊂{Re>0} ⇒ ∮h′/h=0. (Don't match zeros/poles of h — they cancel at shared zeros, e.g. g=f/2.)
Open mapping 3.6.6 Rouché perturbation: |w−w₀|<δ≤|f−w₀| on a small circle ⇒ f−w has as many zeros as f−w₀ inside ⇒ B(w₀,δ)⊆ image. Nonconstant holo on a domain is open.
Inverse function thm 3.6.8 Continuity of f−1 is now free (open mapping); then the 1.4.12 difference-quotient gives g′=1/f′(g). (f′≠0 is automatic for injective holo.)
Residue-calculus toolbox §3.7
Jordan's lemma 3.7.10–12 sin t ≥ 2t/π on [0,π/2] ⇒ |eiαz|=e−αR sin t decays ⇒ arc ∮ f eiαz → 0 if f→0 in UHP. Use eiz (never sin z); take Im at the end.
Indentation lemma 3.7.15 Small arc of angle (β−α) at a simple pole picks up i(β−α)Res — a half-turn = ±πi·Res. (Holo part is O(ε).)
Standard contours §3.7 semicircle (rational) · Jordan (oscillatory ∫sin x/x) · indent (pole on axis) · π cot πz / π csc (sum Σ1/n²) · keyhole (branch cuts, xα).

② Result + proof sketch — same order

Foundations / engines
Estimation (ML) lemma 2.2.8(4) Result: |∮γf dz| ≤ supγ*|f| · ℓ(γ). ∮f = reiθ; take Re of e−iθ∮f, bound by real triangle inequality; sup attained as γ* compact.
FTC / converse 2.2.13, 2.2.16 Result: ∮γf = F(γ(b))−F(γ(a)); for cts f on a domain, primitive exists ⟺ all loop integrals vanish. Chain rule + real FTC on Re/Im. Converse: F(z)=∮z₀→zf well-defined (two paths = a loop, ∮=0); F′=f by appending a segment + continuity.
Winding number 2.5.1, 2.5.4 Result: I(γ,z₀) = a(1)−a(0) ∈ ℤ = 1/2πi ∮γ dz/(z−z₀). Lift γ=|γ|e2πi a: uniform continuity chops [0,1] into half-plane arcs; glue local args (differ by integers). Integral form: integrand = d/dt[log r + 2πi a].
Cauchy-type integrals are analytic Prop 2.5.6 Result: w ↦ 1/2πi ∮ f(z)/(z−w) dz is analytic off γ*, with the n! / (z−z₀)n+1 derivative formula. |w/z|<½ near z₀ ⇒ 1/(z−w)=Σ wⁿ/zn+1; |f|≤M on compact γ* ⇒ M-test ⇒ uniform ⇒ swap Σ↔∮ ⇒ power series in w. The reusable move.
The spine
Cauchy–Goursat (triangles) 2.3.1 Result: f holo ⇒ ∮∂Tf = 0 for any triangle T ⊂ U. Bisect into 4 sub-triangles, keep the one with largest |∮|, iterate → nested triangles shrinking to a point z*; differentiability ε-form makes ∮ = o(diam²·perimeter) → 0.
Cauchy, star-like / Deformation / s.c. 2.4.7, 2.4.9 Result: homotopic paths (rel endpoints) give equal integrals; on s.c. domains ∮=0 and primitives exist. Star-like: F(z)=∮centre→z, Goursat ⇒ F′=f. Deformation: chop homotopy square into cells inside discs (local primitives), telescope. s.c.: null-homotopic ⇒ ∮=0, then 2.2.16.
Cauchy's Integral Formula 3.1.1, 3.1.3 Result: f(w) = 1/2πi ∮γ f(z)/(z−w) dz for w inside γ. Deform γ to a circle of radius ε about w (2.5.10 + deformation); split f(z)=f(w)+(f(z)−f(w)); first term gives f(w)·I=f(w), second → 0 by est. lemma. Multi-curve: cut into a s.c. region.
Taylor: holo ⇒ analytic Cor 3.3.2 Result: f = Σ cn(z−z₀)ⁿ on all of B(z₀,R); cn=f(n)(z₀)/n! = 1/2πi ∮ f/(z−z₀)n+1. CIF writes f as a Cauchy-type integral; Prop 2.5.6 expands it as a power series in w. Two arrows (CIF + 2.5.6) meet here. ⇒ holo ⟺ analytic ⟺ C∞.
Falls out of CIF + Taylor
Liouville 3.3.4 Result: bounded entire ⇒ constant. Two-centre CIF: f(w)−f(0) = 1/2πi ∮|z|=R w f(z)/(z(z−w)); est. lemma ≤ M|w|/(R−|w|) → 0 as R→∞.
Fundamental theorem of algebra 3.3.6 Result: every non-constant p ∈ ℂ[z] has a root (⇒ splits into linear factors). If p≠0 then 1/p entire; |p|≥½|z|ⁿ for |z|≥R ⇒ 1/p bounded ⇒ Liouville ⇒ p constant. ✗.
Morera 3.3.8/9 Result: cts f with ∮∂Tf=0 for all triangles ⇒ f holo (converse of Cauchy). 2.2.16 (locally) ⇒ primitive F; F holo ⇒ C∞ by Taylor ⇒ f=F′ holo. Holomorphy is local, so triangles on balls suffice.
Loc-unif limits stay holo 3.3.12 Result: fn holo → f locally uniformly ⇒ f holo. On a ball ∮γfn=0 (star-like Cauchy); uniform on compact γ* ⇒ ∮f=lim∮fn=0; Morera.
Identity theorem 3.4.1–3.4.3 Result: two holo functions agreeing on a set with a limit point in the (connected) domain agree everywhere. g=f₁−f₂: each zero is isolated or interior (Taylor ⇒ g=(z−z₀)kg₁ or g≡0). int(zero set) is clopen in U; connected ⇒ all of U.
Riemann removable singularity 3.5.21 Result: z₀ removable ⟺ f bounded near z₀. h=(z−z₀)²f, h(z₀)=0: diff-quotient (z−z₀)f → 0 ⇒ h holo with double zero; Taylor ⇒ f = Σk≥0 ak+2(z−z₀)k extends.
Casorati–Weierstrass 3.5.25 Result: near an essential singularity, the image is dense in ℂ. If f misses B(z₀,δ), then 1/(f−z₀) bounded ⇒ removable (Riemann) ⇒ a is a pole/removable, not essential. ✗.
Laurent's theorem 3.5.9 Result: on an annulus, f = Σn=−∞∞ cn(z−z₀)ⁿ, cn=1/2πi ∮ f/(z−z₀)n+1. Two-circle CIF (3.1.3): f(w)=(outer ∮)−(inner ∮); expand outer in (z−z₀)− powers, inner in (z−z₀)+ powers via geometric series + M-test.
Residue theorem 3.5.27 Result: 1/2πi ∮γf = Σa I(γ,a) Resaf. g = f − Σ principal parts has only removable singularities ⇒ holo ⇒ ∮γg=0 (γ null-homotopic). Each Pa converges uniformly on γ*; only c−1/(z−a) lacks a primitive ⇒ gives 2πi·I·Res.
Argument-principle family
Argument principle 3.6.2 Result: N−P = 1/2πi ∮γ f′/f = I(f∘γ, 0) (zeros − poles inside, with multiplicity). Residue theorem on f′/f: by Lem 3.6.1 it has simple poles with residues = orders ±k. Last equality: substitute w=f(γ(t)) in ∮ dw/w.
Rouché 3.6.3 Result: |f|>|g| on γ* ⇒ f and f+g have equally many zeros inside γ. On γ*: f≠0, f+g≠0 ⇒ arg principle gives Nf, Nf+g separately. Quotient rule: h′/h = (f+g)′/(f+g) − f′/f with h=(f+g)/f, so Nf+g−Nf = 1/2πi ∮ h′/h; |g/f|<1 ⇒ h(γ)⊂B(1,1)⊂{Re>0} ⇒ ∮h′/h=0. Count f, f+g separately — never match zeros/poles of h (cancel at shared zeros: g=f/2 ⇒ h≡3/2).
Open mapping theorem 3.6.6/7 Result: non-constant holo on a domain is an open map; order-d zero of f−w₀ ⇒ locally d-to-1. g=f−w₀ has isolated zero at z₀; |g|≥δ on a small circle. For |w−w₀|<δ: |w₀−w|<|g| ⇒ Rouché ⇒ f−w has a zero inside ⇒ B(w₀,δ) ⊆ f(B(z₀,r)).
Inverse function theorem 3.6.8/9 Result: injective holo f with f′≠0 ⇒ f−1 holo, (f−1)′(w)=1/f′(f−1(w)). Continuity of f−1 is free: (f−1)−1(V)=f(V) open by open mapping. Then 1.4.12's difference-quotient finishes. (Injective holo ⇒ f′≠0 automatically.)
Residue-calculus toolbox §3.7
Jordan's lemma 3.7.10–12 Result: |∮γ_R f eiαz| ≤ (π/α) maxγ_R|f| → 0 if f→0 in the UHP (α>0). sin t ≥ 2t/π on [0,π/2] (concavity) ⇒ |eiαz|=e−αR sin t ≤ e−2αRt/π; integrate. α<0 ⇒ use LHP. Take Im at the end; never integrate sin z (blows up upward).
Indentation (pole on contour) 3.7.15 Result: small arc of angle (β−α) at a simple pole a: ∮γ_εf → i(β−α)Resaf. f = c/(z−a) + g (g holo, bounded ⇒ O(ε) by est. lemma); the c/(z−a) term integrates exactly to ic(β−α). Half-turn ⇒ ±πi·Res.
